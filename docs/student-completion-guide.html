Student workflow

What students do, in order

01

Sign in

Use the course account provided by the instructor or continuing education unit. Signed-in progress is required for official evidence review and certificate issuance.

02

Review the credential

Open the credential page, confirm duration, CEU value, evidence requirements, and the portfolio products expected at the end.

03

Learn through 12 modules

Move through the complete pathway: framing, learner context, crosswalk, mechanics, facilitation, prototype, interaction spec, playtest, audit, revision, and final defense.

04

Submit evidence

Use the final submission editor to assemble context, alignment, GenAI design documentation, evidence, and reflection into one reviewable packet.

Completion model

How certificate handoff works

Official learner path

  • Student signs in with a course account.
  • Student completes the guided course and portfolio worksheets.
  • Student submits evidence for instructor review.
  • Instructor approves the submission against the rubric.
  • TeachPlay exposes the certificate and credential evidence.

Preview mode note

The screen recording uses a demo learner path so the orientation video can show the full journey without exposing a real student account. The certificate shown in preview mode is clearly labeled and points to sample credential JSON.

Instructor setup

What to tell students before they start

Minimum instructions

  • Use the same email account for every TeachPlay session.
  • Do not submit the final packet until all required evidence artifacts are ready.
  • Keep AI prompts, outputs, edits, and rejected ideas in the provenance log.
  • Use the 12-module sequence as the learning path. Use the portfolio checkpoints for evidence submission and completion review.

Evidence packet

  • D1 learner/context and learning problem
  • D2 objective-to-mechanic crosswalk
  • D3 prototype and facilitation guide
  • D4 playtest and audit evidence
  • D5 final defense and implementation packet

FAQ

Common student questions

Do I need to code a full game?

No. A paper prototype, low-code prototype, or implementation specification can satisfy the credential if the learning loop, facilitation logic, AI governance, playtest evidence, and revision rationale are reviewable.

Why do I see 12 modules and portfolio checkpoints?

The 12-module sequence is the learning path. Portfolio checkpoints collect the evidence students produce across those modules for submission, review, and certificate readiness.

When do I receive the certificate?

The official certificate is available after authenticated submission and instructor approval. Preview mode can show the handoff screen for orientation, but it is not an official award.

What should I do if my certificate says not found?

First confirm that you are signed in with the same account used for the course. Then confirm that your final evidence packet was submitted and approved. If both are true, contact the instructor or program administrator.
