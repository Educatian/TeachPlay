First, create your TeachPlay learner account. Use the name and email you want on your credential, because that is what keeps your progress and certificate record connected.
Once you are signed in, open Session 01. The rhythm is pretty simple: read the short guide, try the activity, save anything you need, and mark the session complete when you are done.
TeachPlay has twelve modules. The portfolio checkpoints are not extra busywork. They are where you collect the evidence you are already building as you move through the course.
As you work, TeachPlay saves progress in the browser and sends completion events when you are signed in. So if you come back later, the dashboard can pick up the pathway again.
After you finish all twelve sessions, go to Session 12 and open the credential request form. Use the same name and email from your account so the review record matches.
Submitting the form sends your evidence packet to instructor review. It is not an instant auto-certificate, and that is intentional. The review step is what makes the microcredential worth trusting.
After approval, you receive the certificate handoff and the signed credential evidence. So the full path is: create an account, work through the modules, submit your evidence, pass review, and claim the certificate.
