TeachPlay Avatar Mini-Lecture: Prototype v1 and Provenance Log

Prototype v1 does not need to be polished. It needs to make your design claim playable.

Keep the provenance log open while you build. Do not wait until the end and try to reconstruct everything.

When AI suggests a rule, asset, dialogue, or mechanic, write down what you kept, changed, or rejected.

Also record the risk checks. Look for confusing feedback, bias, hallucinated content, and misalignment with the learning goal.

Your playable loop should let someone experience the target learning action, even if the visuals are still rough.

The log helps a reviewer understand how the artifact was made and why your choices are defensible.

Once the loop works, you are ready for the next question: what happens when a learner actually plays it?
