TeachPlay Avatar Mini-Lecture: Playtest Evidence and Revision

A playtest does not have to be big. It has to give you evidence you can actually use.

Before anyone plays, define the task, the observable traces, and one debrief question that really matters.

In Space Invaders, shot angle, trajectory revision, gravity-assist hits, and misses can point to physics reasoning.

In Chalk and Chance, the debrief can focus on teaching moves like elicit, extend, redirect, wait, or revoice.

Do not collect data just because the system can log it. Every trace should help you make a revision decision.

Rank revisions by learning impact. A clearer feedback message may matter more than a huge new feature.

Your final portfolio should show what changed after the playtest and why that change should improve learning.
