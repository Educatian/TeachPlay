TeachPlay Avatar Mini-Lecture: Governance Packet and Final Defense

Your final submission is not just a folder of files. It is a design argument someone can review.

Start with the learning problem. Who are the learners, and what performance should become stronger?

Then show the mechanic. How does the player action carry the learning objective?

Bring in the AI governance trail. What did AI help with, what did you edit, and what risks did you check?

Use playtest evidence to explain what changed. Do not just summarize feedback; connect evidence to revision.

Organize the packet so the rubric is easy to verify: alignment, provenance, evidence quality, and revision rationale.

The goal is not a perfect commercial game. The goal is a serious learning-game prototype an educator can defend.
