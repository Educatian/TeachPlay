TeachPlay Avatar Mini-Lecture: From Objective to Mechanic

Welcome to TeachPlay. Here is the big idea: your game starts with the learning objective, not with the genre.

A serious learning game works when the player action matches the thinking you want students to practice.

So if students need to compare evidence, make them compare evidence. If they need to revise, build revision into the loop.

That is what the objective-to-mechanic crosswalk does. It connects the objective, action, feedback, and evidence claim.

You will see this in Space Invaders and Chalk and Chance. The playable artifact matters, but the design record matters too.

By the end, your portfolio should show what was learned, how AI helped, what humans decided, and what evidence supports revision.

Use the worksheets as you go. They are not busywork. They become the backbone of your final submission.
