TeachPlay Avatar Mini-Lecture: Scope a Five-Minute Play Loop

A five-minute loop is not a tiny version of the whole game. It is the smallest cycle that can prove the learning idea works.

Start with a prediction. Ask the learner what they think will happen before they act.

Then make the action matter. The player should have to use the target concept, not just click through content.

Next, make feedback readable. The learner should see what changed and why it changed.

Then create a chance to revise. That second try is where learning becomes much easier to see.

Cut the extra systems for now. Bosses, upgrades, cosmetics, and side quests can wait until the core loop teaches.

At the end, you should be able to say exactly what your five-minute loop teaches and what evidence it creates.
